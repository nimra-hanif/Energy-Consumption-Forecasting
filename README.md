# Task Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze historical household energy consumption data and develop accurate time series forecasting models to predict future electricity usage. The project focuses on preprocessing time-based data, performing exploratory data analysis, engineering meaningful temporal features, and comparing different forecasting techniques including ARIMA, Prophet, and XGBoost. The goal is to identify the most effective model for forecasting energy consumption patterns and to gain insights into electricity usage behavior over time.
# Approach
The project began with data preprocessing and cleaning, where missing values and incorrect data entries were handled to ensure data quality. The date and time columns were combined into a single datetime feature and resampled into meaningful time intervals for analysis. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was then performed to understand trends, seasonality, correlations, and energy consumption patterns. Time-based features such as hour of day, weekdays, and weekend indicators were engineered to improve forecasting performance. Three forecasting models — ARIMA, Prophet, and XGBoost — were trained and evaluated using performance metrics such as MAE and RMSE. Finally, actual and forecasted energy usage values were visualized to compare the prediction accuracy of each model.
# Results and Findings
The analysis showed that household energy consumption follows clear daily and weekly patterns, with noticeable variations across different times of the day and weekends. The correlation analysis revealed strong relationships between global power consumption and electrical intensity-related variables. Among the forecasting models, ARIMA performed well for capturing linear trends, Prophet effectively handled seasonal behavior, and XGBoost demonstrated strong predictive capability for complex non-linear patterns when using engineered features. Visualization of actual versus predicted values highlighted that machine learning-based models can provide highly accurate forecasts for energy consumption data. Overall, the project demonstrates the effectiveness of time series forecasting techniques in improving energy demand prediction and supporting efficient energy management decisions.
